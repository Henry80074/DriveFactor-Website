How it works

The Traffic Scenario Planner draws on data from sensor networks to model the relationship between traffic flow conditions and collision likelihood across each road segment. Rather than treating risk as static, it captures how congestion, speed deviation and unexpected changes in traffic patterns each contribute to elevated risk on specific corridors.

Using the planner, you can simulate a range of traffic conditions and see immediately how risk responds across the network. This makes it straightforward to answer questions that would otherwise require a full modelling exercise: how does the risk profile of a key corridor change when traffic volumes rise above typical levels? What is the estimated safety impact of a diversion that reroutes vehicles onto roads operating outside their normal flow range? Where does risk concentrate during a congested peak period?

Each segment is scored against its own baseline, so the planner identifies where conditions are creating anomalous risk rather than simply flagging busy roads. This gives road safety teams and transport planners a reliable basis for prioritising monitoring, informing operational decisions and making the case for targeted interventions.
