How the planner works

The scenario planner evaluates the L2 × L3 risk model at hypothetical input values rather than re-running any simulation. Speed changes are converted to per-link z-scores using historical IQR values, which are then passed through the L3 GAM to produce odds ratios. Weather conditions update the L2 multiplier via a precomputed lookup table.

The combined score — L2 × L3, capped at 8× — is applied to each segment and the map updates immediately. This means planners can explore thousands of scenarios without running new traffic models.

Typical applications include assessing how a congested PM peak with rain changes the risk profile of a key corridor, or quantifying the risk benefit of a 10mph speed reduction on a particular route.
