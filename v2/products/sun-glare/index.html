Sun Glare Intensity Maps

Solar trajectory modelling matched to road bearing. Identifies which segments face direct sun exposure by time of day and season, and flags periods of severe glare risk.

What the map shows

The map plots sun glare exposure across the road network at each 15-minute window of the day. Solar azimuth is calculated using the Astral library at network centroid coordinates, then compared against the road bearing of each OSM segment.

When the difference between solar azimuth and road bearing falls within ±5°, a driver travelling into the sun faces direct glare. The map colours segments by their worst-case glare intensity across the selected time window.

Seasonal and daily patterns are built in — the AM peak in November and February produces particularly acute westward glare on east-facing routes. The time slider allows exploration of how glare windows shift across the day.

Sun glare is associated with a 2–4× uplift in crash risk in Stats19 analysis of bright/glare conditions. This layer can be overlaid with spatial risk scores to identify high-priority segments.
