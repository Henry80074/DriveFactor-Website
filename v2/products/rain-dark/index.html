What the map shows

This map shows absolute crash risk per million vehicle kilometres travelled (MVKT) — the probability that a vehicle travelling along a given road segment will be involved in a collision. By dividing modelled annual crashes by total vehicle exposure (AADF × segment length × 365 days), it removes the confounding effect of traffic volume.

A busy A-road with 50 crashes a year and 10 million VKT has a lower risk per VKT than a quiet rural B-road with 5 crashes and only 100k VKT. This measure identifies roads that are genuinely dangerous to use, not simply roads that carry a lot of traffic.

The metric is directly comparable to national benchmarks published by DfT and used in Safe System approaches to road design. It is the correct input for prioritising capital investment schemes and safety camera deployments where reducing risk-per-journey is the objective.

Combined with Dynamic Road Risk modelling, the per-VKT baseline enables route safety optimisation and dynamic departure scheduling — selecting the lowest-risk path or departure time based on real-time conditions, diurnal flow profiles, and weather multipliers. This makes Driver Road Risk a core input for fleet safety systems, logistics routing engines, and connected vehicle platforms seeking to minimise exposure-weighted crash probability across a journey.
