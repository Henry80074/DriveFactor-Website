What the map shows

Each segment is coloured by its L3 crash odds ratio — the modelled likelihood of a crash during this interval relative to the annual average for that road type and time of day. Ratios above 1.0× indicate elevated risk; below 1.0× indicates below-average conditions.

The L3 model converts observed or modelled traffic speed into a within-stratum z-score using per-link hourly IQR values derived from historical sensor data. That z-score is passed through a varying-coefficient cyclic cubic spline GAM to produce a smooth odds ratio estimate.

The map shown here is scored from Aimsun APM (Assignment with Performance Monitoring) outputs. The same model can be applied to live WebTRIS data on a 15-minute update cycle.

Model validation on held-out Tees Valley intervals: AUC 0.762. Leave-road-out AUC 0.732, confirming the model generalises beyond the training roads.
