Road Safety Intervention Planner

Click any 30 mph road segment to see the predicted crash reduction from a 30→20 mph speed limit change. Powered by a causal forest trained on staggered difference-in-differences data from Wales, London, Scotland and England corridors, with Wales k-NN group-level fallback.

What the map shows

Each green road segment is a 30 mph road in Stockton-on-Tees. The colour intensity shows the predicted percentage reduction in crashes if the speed limit were changed to 20 mph — darker green means a larger modelled benefit.

Predictions use a three-tier hierarchy. Where a segment was part of the staggered DiD training set, a direct causal forest CATE estimate is used. For all other segments, a Random Forest model trained on road geometry, traffic volume, junction density, population density and network neighbourhood features produces a segment-level log risk-ratio. Both tiers are shrunk toward the Wales DiD group-level average treatment effect (~20%) to prevent overfitting on high-crash outlier segments. Where neither model has confidence, the Wales k-NN group-level DiD provides a safe fallback stratified by road class, lane count, and population density tertile.

Crash counts span 2014–2024 (excluding 2020–21 as COVID disrupted traffic patterns), giving 9 clean years of Stats19 matched to OSM 2025 segments. The pre-intervention crash rate is computed as annual crashes per km, length-normalised and used as the primary model feature alongside AADF, junction density, and network context.

Live Risk Alerts

15-minute L3 risk scores computed from WebTRIS inductive loop sensor data. Flags corridors where current traffic conditions are pushing crash risk significantly above the time-of-day baseline.

What the map shows

The PM alert map shows each monitored segment scored at the most recent 15-minute sensor interval. Segments are coloured by their L3 odds ratio — how much the current traffic state elevates crash probability above the per-hour, per-road-type baseline.

WebTRIS loop sensors provide volume and average speed at 15-minute resolution. The L3 model converts this to a within-stratum z-score and then to a crash odds ratio using per-clock-hour GAM splines with natural-anchor calibration (offset = 0.7327).

When the combined L2 × L3 score on a monitored corridor exceeds a configurable threshold — typically 3× — an alert is generated and surfaced in the map and via integration with Confluence or traffic management systems.

Alert thresholds and corridor subscriptions are configurable per deployment. Integration with Atlassian Confluence and WebTRIS API is included in the standard package.
