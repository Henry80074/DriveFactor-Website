Spatial Risk Maps

Crash probability per road segment, derived from road geometry, junction structure, speed limit, road type and historical crash density. The static baseline layer of the DriveFactor risk engine.

What the map shows

Each road segment is coloured by its modelled crash probability relative to the network average. Segments absorb crash history from Stats19 records matched to OSM geometry via spatial join and nearest-link assignment.

Colour thresholds are network-relative: red segments sit in the top 5% of predicted crash probability on this network. This makes the map directly comparable across routes and useful for prioritising site investigations.

The map can be filtered by road type, speed limit band, or severity threshold. Underlying segment data is exportable as GeoJSON or CSV for integration with GIS workflows.

This layer provides the L1 spatial baseline. It does not change with time of day or traffic conditions — for dynamic scoring, see the Dynamic Traffic Risk product.
