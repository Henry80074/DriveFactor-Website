Methodology

A multi-layer risk architecture

Risk on any road is shaped by many interacting factors. DriveFactor brings all of them together, combining crash history, physical road characteristics, environmental conditions and traffic behaviour into a single risk score for every segment, at every hour of the day.

L1

Road environment & crash history

Structural risk grounded in road geometry, junction type, speed limit, carriageway layout and over a decade of Stats19 recorded collision data, scored to individual road segments, not zones or wards.

Road geometry · Stats19 · OSM
L2

Temporal & environmental conditions

Risk varies significantly by time of day, day of week, season and weather. Fatigue-related patterns, darkness, precipitation, wind and other conditions known to influence crash likelihood are all considered.

Hour · Season · Weather · Darkness
L3

Traffic volume & speed behaviour

Measured and modelled traffic conditions, including flow estimates, speeds and driving behaviour, converting static risk into a dynamic, actionable score.

Traffic Volume · Speed · Flow estimates
