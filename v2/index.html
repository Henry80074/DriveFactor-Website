Methodology

A three-layer risk architecture

Each layer addresses a different time horizon of crash risk. They multiply together to produce a combined score.

L1

Spatial baseline — road geometry and crash history

XGBoost model trained on OpenStreetMap geometry features and historic Stats19 crash rates per road segment. Produces a static per-segment baseline risk score capturing where crashes cluster structurally.

XGBoost
OSM + Stats19
L2

Temporal & weather — when and under what conditions

Penalised Poisson GAM trained on 560,000 Stats19 crashes (2013–2023), AADF-weighted. Models crash rate as a function of hour, day type, month, precipitation and wind gust. Typical multiplier range 0.4–4.0×.

0.4–4.0×
Multiplier range
L3

Live traffic — speed deviation from per-link baselines

Varying-coefficient cyclic cubic spline GAM trained on 814,700 WebTRIS sensor intervals. Converts within-stratum speed z-score to crash odds ratio. AUC 0.762 on held-out intervals. Up to 11× OR at −1 IQR speed deviation.
