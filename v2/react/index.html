Methodology

How the Scenario Planner works

The planner evaluates the DriveFactor crash risk models at hypothetical input points — fast lookup and interpolation against pre-trained model outputs. No simulation, no re-fitting.

Convert speed to z-score

Your speed delta (mph) is divided by the historical IQR for that road type and hour band — converting an absolute change into a normalised deviation relative to what that road normally does.

Evaluate L3 odds ratio

A varying-coefficient cyclic cubic spline GAM returns the crash odds ratio for that z-score at the current time of day. The smooth f(t) function ensures no discontinuities at hour boundaries.

Look up L2 multiplier

The temporal + weather multiplier is retrieved from a precomputed 120,960-row lookup grid covering all combinations of road type, day type, month, hour, precipitation bin, and wind gust bin.

Combine and display

Combined = L2 × L3, capped at 8×. The breakdown shows which layer dominates. The sensitivity chart sweeps z-score from −2 IQR to +2 IQR to show how responsive this road type is to speed deviation.
